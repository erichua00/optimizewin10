Optimize Windows 10 Performance

(Especially for Laptops with Nvidia Graphics Card)

2. Optimize Graphics Settings

Geforce Experience is a software provided by Nvidia which can update your Nvidia graphics card driver to the latest version.

First, Geforce Experience need to be downloaded and installed.

Then, you can update graphics driver by opening Geforce Experience then proceed to Drivers -> Download.

After finish downloading the latest driver, click "Express Installation" and let the progress to be completed.

You can optimize a game graphics by opening Geforce Experience, click on a game profile then click "Optimize".

Recommend to turn off Nvidia Shield, Whisper Mode, Battery Boost and In-game Overlay.

If you are using these features you can leave them turn on.

Nvidia Shield: For streaming use.

Whisper Mode: Ensure a quieter gaming by lowering the RPM of the fans.

Battery Boost: Extend your battery life while gaming.

In-game Overlay: Provide in-game overlay to capture and share the videos, take screenshots and stream live with friends

Note that turning on these features on may affect your gameplay if you're not using a high-end laptop.

You should be able to find Nvidia Control Panel after finish installing Geforce Experience and updating graphics driver.

1. Open Nvidia Control Panel:

Search and open Nvidia Control Panel from the start or open it from the dekstop context menu.



2. Restore to Default Settings:

First, in Nvidia Control Panel proceed to Manage 3D settings -> Global Settings, click"Restore" then click "Yes".

Click "Apply" at the right side bottom to apply the settings.



3. Custom Program Settings:

Proceed to the Program Settings Tab, you can select your game and change the graphics settings here.

As an example, Apex Legends is selected here. If your graphics card cannot support up to the 1920*1080 resolution and making your game blurry, you can apply Image Sharpening here.

Under Image Sharpening select On, change Sharpen and Ignore film grain to the value 1.0. Press "Ok" then click "Apply" to apply the settings.

Leave all the settings to be default if your graphics card can support up to 1920*1080 resolution of the game.



Now you're finished with the Nvidia Control Panel settings.
